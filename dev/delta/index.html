δ-Distributions

Maximum likelihood (ML) and Maximum A Posteriori inference are special cases of Bayesian inference where the posterior is assumed to be a Dirac δ distribution:

\[\begin{aligned} \delta_{\mu}(x) &= \delta(x - \mu) = \begin{cases} \infty,& \text{if } x - \mu = 0 \\ 0 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases} \\ \int_x \delta_{\mu}(x) \text{d}x &= 1 \end{aligned}\]

To easily switch between Bayesian inference ML/MAP, the package provides the δ-distributions, i.e. Dirac δ dsistribution wrap around an "equivalent" distribution member of the exponential family.

ExpFamilyDistributions.δDistribution — Type
abstract type δDistribution

Supertype for the δ-distributions.

source

Each subtype of δDistribution implements partially the Exponential Family interface:

  • gradlognorm
  • mean
  • sample
  • splitgrad
  • update!.

δ-Normal distribution

ExpFamilyDistributions.δNormal — Type
mutable struct δNormal{T,D} <: δDistribution
    μ
end

The δ-equivalent of the Normal distribution.

Constructors

δNormal{T,D}()
δNormal(μ)

where T is the encoding type of the parameters and D is the dimension of the support and μ is the location of the Dirac δ pulse.

Examples

julia> δNormal{Float32,2}()
δNormal{Float32,2}:
  μ = Float32[0.0, 0.0]

julia> δNormal([1.0, 1.0])
δNormal{Float64,2}:
  μ = [1.0, 1.0]
